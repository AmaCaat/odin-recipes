Pulpo a feira

Pulpo a feira also known as Polbo รก feira is a one of the spanish famous tradition foods and it means "fair-style octopus". Tough it was originally from Galicia, Spain, it got spread by way its delicious and unique taste and now is popular and eaten troughout the whole country.

This is very easy to make and with only few ingredients.

Recipe tutorial

Frozen octopus works particularly well, since the freezing tenderizes the otherwise quite tough meat, so that is what I suggest you use here. If you prefer to use a fresh octopus, you will need to practice the almost tribal tradition of beating the animal with a rock or meat mallet to rid of its rubbery texture. Another option is to freeze fresh octopus for about two weeks to tenderize it.

  • Prepare octopus and cut into pieces
  • Boil a large stockpot and add salt
  • Dip octopus to the boiling water and take it out so it pot return to boil (4 times)
  • Then submerge the octopus and let it be for 2 hours until its tender
  • Turn off heat and let the octopus take a breath for about 10 minutes
  • Divide the octopus pieces and potatoes evenly among 6 plates
  • Drizzle them with paprika, olive oil and you can add some spices of your choice to finish the taste.
  • Eat when octopus and potatoes still warm

    • Ingredients

  • 4 quarts water
  • frozen octopus (frozen for atleast 1 day)
  • some salt
  • cup of olive oil
  • pound new potatoes (small potatos)
  • tea spoon of paprika
